# Research Repository: Green Energy Lab

## 1. Abstract
The Green Energy Lab Research Repository is a centralized collection of research notes, datasets, experiment results, and peer-reviewed findings regarding renewable energy technologies. All contributions maintain a transparent history to ensure reproducibility and foster open scientific collaboration.

## 2. Current Focus Areas
Our current research initiatives are divided into three primary categories:
- **Solar Photovoltaics:** Enhancing the efficiency of perovskite solar cells under high-temperature conditions.
- **Wind Turbine Aerodynamics:** Analyzing blade wear and tear in offshore environments using predictive modeling.
- **Energy Storage:** Developing solid-state battery prototypes with increased energy density and reduced degradation over time.

## 3. Datasets and Experiment Logs
All raw datasets and experiment logs are stored in the `/data` directory. 
- **Data Formatting:** Ensure all CSV files use UTF-8 encoding. Time-series data must follow the ISO 8601 standard format.
- **Logging Protocols:** Every experiment must be documented using the standard `Experiment_Log_Template.md`. Include the hypothesis, methodology, control variables, and preliminary results.
- **Version Control:** Datasets are versioned. Do not overwrite existing data files; instead, append a version number (e.g., `solar_irradiance_v2.csv`) and update the `changelog.txt`.

## 4. Findings & Publications
Completed research ready for peer review should be submitted to the `publications` directory.
- Drafts should be formatted using our LaTeX template or as standard Markdown files with mathematical formulas enclosed in LaTeX syntax (`$$...$$`).
- All community members are encouraged to review open drafts and provide constructive feedback via pull requests.

## 5. How to Contribute Data
We welcome contributions from independent researchers and institutions. To contribute data:
1. Ensure your data collection methods adhere to our ethical and environmental guidelines.
2. Clean your data and format it according to our standards outlined in Section 3.
3. Open a pull request with a detailed description of the dataset, collection methodology, and any potential biases or limitations.
